Introduction

Every day, we encounter numerous faces. They appear on the screens of our phones, social media platforms, and in our workplaces, homes, trams, and parks. Human faces are such a ubiquitous visual element that we often take how our brains recognize and identify them for granted. Cognitive science and neuroscience attempt to elucidate this process and explain how our brains distinguish and tailor faces to specific individuals. With technological advancements, we have taught artificial intelligence to recognize and verify human faces. Consequently, facial biometric technology has become one of the most prevalent methods for identity verification services today.

Use Cases

Face verification is often used as a security measure to prevent unauthorized access to systems or services. It is commonly used for mobile device authentication, access control for buildings and secure areas, airport passport control, airplane boarding procedures, online identity verification, and many more applications. It is considered a highly accurate and secure authentication method, as each person's face is unique and challenging to replicate.

How does the survey work?

Each survey consists of several questions. The questions are always the same, but the images are different. You have to decide whether the two images are of the same person. The images are presented in pairs; you must decide for each pair. You should specify your certainty level with a slider in addition to your answer. You can leave the survey anytime by clicking the "Exit" button at the bottom. Your current progress is saved, and you can continue later.
